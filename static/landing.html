I The Attention Economy Your focus was sold before you finished reading this sentence. The product was never the page. It was the pause between your thoughts.
II The Infinite Scroll There was once a bottom to a page. A place where you chose to stop. That moment of closure was taken from you by design.
III Dark Patterns Consent was manufactured through friction. The decline button was grey and small and three clicks deep. This was not accident. This was architecture.
IV Surveillance Without Witness Behaviour was collected before it was named. Sold to someone who will use it without ever knowing your face. You were observed by no one and tracked by everything.
V The Algorithmic Tunnel Reality was curated by engagement. What provoked a reaction was elevated. The world you saw was the world that kept you clicking. The rest was rendered invisible.
VI The Cloud as Landlord Your words live in a data center you will never visit, in a jurisdiction you did not choose. You are a tenant. Your files are collateral.
VII Five Companies, One Internet The web was built as a garden of many voices. It was gradually purchased and walled. None of them owe you anything.
VIII The Cookie Banner Theater The options were arranged so that the answer that served you was buried, and the answer that served them was immediate. This was called compliance.
IX Knowledge Shaped by Ranking What was true mattered less than what was indexed. A generation learned to search instead of to know.
X The Notification as Colonisation They asked permission to interrupt you. You gave it once. It became a lease renewed without asking. Your mornings were not your own.
XI Four Megabytes to Render a Paragraph What could be sent in twelve kilobytes arrived in four megabytes, and the page still took three seconds to become readable. This was called progress.
XII The Terms of Service as Surrender You agreed to things that had not been written yet. Rights were transferred in the scrolling. This was called agreement.
XIII The Murder of the Personal Website Those places were abandoned one by one as the platforms promised ease and delivered homogeneity. The personal web went quiet.
